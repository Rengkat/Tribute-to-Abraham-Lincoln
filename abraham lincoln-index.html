Timeline

A chronology of key events in the life of Abraham Lincoln (1809-1865), lawyer, representative from Illinois, and sixteenth president of the United States. For a more detailed chronology, consult The Lincoln Log External or the three-volume Lincoln Day by Day: A Chronology, 1809-1865, edited by Earl Schenck Miers (Washington, D.C., 1960) and available online through HathiTrust External.

1809, Feb. 12 Born in Hardin County (now LaRue County), Kentucky, to Thomas Lincoln (1778-1851) and Nancy Hanks Lincoln (1784-1818)

1832, Apr. 7 Elected captain, Thirty-first Regiment, Illinois Militia, in the Black Hawk War

1832, Aug. 6 Defeated in election for a seat in the Illinois General Assembly

1834, Aug. 4 Elected as Whig candidate from Sangamon County to the lower house of the Illinois General Assembly, which then met in Vandalia, Illinois

1838, Jan. 27 Delivered address on "The Perpetuation of Our Political Institutions" to Young Men's Lyceum of Springfield

1842, Nov. 4 Married to Mary Todd (1818-1882), daughter of Robert Smith Todd (1791-1849) and Elizabeth Parker Todd (1794-1825) of Lexington, Kentucky

1846, Aug. 3 Elected from the Whig Party to the United States House of Representatives; assumed office in December 1847

1849, Sept. Declined appointment to be governor of the Oregon Territory

1856, June 19 Received 110 votes for vice president at the Republican National Convention in Philadelphia; William L. Dayton of New Jersey ultimately nominated as vice presidential candidate.

1860, May 18 Nominated as Republican Party's candidate for president

1860, Nov. 6 Elected president of the United States

1861, Apr. 15 Issued proclamation calling for 75,000 volunteers to quell rebellion

1861, July 21 Union forces defeated in first major land battle of the Civil War fought at Manassas, Virginia

1863, Jan. 1 Issued Final Emancipation Proclamation

1864, Nov. 8 Reelected president on November 8

1865, Apr. 14 Shot while attending a performance of "Our American Cousin" at Ford's Theatre. Removed to the Petersen boarding house across the street from the theater.

1865, Apr. 15 Died, 7:22 a.m.
